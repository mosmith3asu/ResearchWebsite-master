Preditor-Prey Game:

Description: You and your AI-partner are predators that must chase and catch your prey around the 2D world. In a series of turn-based games, you must coordinate your actions with your partner in order to catch your prey all while avoiding penalty states to maximize your final score. The purpose of this game is to observe how you and your partner respond to risk and uncertainty when jointly planning.

Duration:

  • 15 to 25 minutes to complete

Requirements:

  • Be between 18-65 years old
  • Playing on a computer (no phone)
  • Have access to keyboard and mouse
  • Be in a distraction-free environment

In this experiment, you will:

  • Complete a pre-trial survey
  • Read instructions and practice controls in a warmup game
  • Complete a series of 7 trials where each trial is composed of:
    • Reading simple trial-specific instructions
    • Playing this trial's game
    • Taking a brief post-trial survey on your attitude towards your partner
  • Be debriefed on your participation in the experiemnt
  • (Optional) Read more about the experiment

Team Assembly Game:

Description: You and your AI-partner are tasked with using a set of blocks to assemble different 2D structures. You will take turns placing, moving, or removing blocks until either your team successfully assemble the assigned structure. This game is designed to look at how your AI partner can model heuristic and short-term plans such that it can best help you achieve your goals.

Duration:

  • 20 to 30 minutes to complete

Requirements:

  • Be between 18-65 years old
  • Playing on a computer (no phone)
  • Have access to keyboard and mouse
  • Be in a distraction-free environment

In this experiment, you will:

  • Complete a pre-trial survey
  • Read instructions and practice controls in a warmup game
  • Complete a series of 3 trials where each trial is composed of:
    • A practice round where you assemble the structure by yourself
    • A collaborative round where you assemble the structure with your partner
    • A brief post-trial survey on your attitude towards your partner
  • Be debriefed on your participation in the experiemnt
  • (Optional) Read more about the experiment
